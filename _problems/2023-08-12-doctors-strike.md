## Summary

The Joint National Association of Residents and Medical Officers (JUMOG) has embarked on a warning strike over deteriorating working conditions in public hospitals. The action threatens to shut down healthcare delivery in teaching hospitals across the country.

## Key Points

- Resident doctors demand implementation of 2021 agreement on allowances
- Hazard duty pay and risk allowances remain unpaid for over 18 months
- Medical equipment in teaching hospitals reported to be over 80% non-functional
- Over 2,500 Nigerian doctors emigrated in 2022 alone (JAPA syndrome)
- Patient-to-doctor ratio now 1:5,000 vs WHO recommendation of 1:600

## Impact

The healthcare workforce crisis affects millions:

- **Patient Care**: Elective surgeries cancelled, emergency care strained
- **Medical Training**: Resident doctor training programs disrupted
- **Brain Drain**: Loss of experienced medical professionals to UK, Canada, Saudi Arabia
- **Public Trust**: Increasing reliance on expensive private healthcare

## Context

Nigeria's healthcare system faces a perfect storm: underfunding, poor infrastructure, and mass emigration of medical professionals. The country spends less than 5% of its budget on health, far below the WHO-recommended 15%.
