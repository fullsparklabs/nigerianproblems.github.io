## Summary

Nigeria's 2023 general elections were marred by technical failures of the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS), delayed result transmission, and widespread allegations of irregularities. The controversies have sparked calls for comprehensive electoral reform.

## Key Points

- BVAS machines failed in numerous polling units across multiple states
- IReV (Result Viewing Portal) experienced upload delays and discrepancies
- Voter turnout remained low at approximately 27% of registered voters
- International observers noted significant procedural irregularities
- Multiple court cases challenging election results in several states

## Impact

The electoral controversies have far-reaching implications:

- **Democratic Trust**: Public confidence in electoral processes eroded
- **Governance**: Elected officials face legitimacy questions
- **Youth Engagement**: #EndSARS generation's political participation dampened
- **International Standing**: Nigeria's democratic reputation affected

## Context

Nigeria's electoral journey has been marked by gradual improvements since 1999. The 2023 elections introduced new technology but also revealed systemic vulnerabilities. The Electoral Act 2022 was intended to strengthen the process, but implementation gaps remain significant.
