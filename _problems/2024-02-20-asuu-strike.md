## Summary

The Academic Staff Union of Universities (ASUU) has announced an indefinite strike action affecting all 36 federal universities across Nigeria. The strike, which began on February 20, 2024, centers on the federal government's failure to implement the 2022 agreement on university funding and welfare improvements.

## Key Points

- Over 800,000 students affected by the indefinite strike action
- Key demands include implementation of revitalization funds, salary adjustments, and earned allowances
- This marks the 8th ASUU strike in the past 15 years
- Union cites government's failure to honor previous MoU signed in 2022
- Negotiations have stalled over funding commitments and timeline for implementation

## Impact

The strike has far-reaching consequences for Nigeria's education sector:

- **Students**: Academic calendar disrupted, with final year students facing graduation delays
- **Research**: Ongoing research projects in federal universities have been put on hold
- **Economy**: Brain drain accelerates as academics seek opportunities abroad
- **Social**: Increased pressure on private universities and foreign institutions

## Context

ASUU strikes have become a recurring feature of Nigeria's educational landscape. Since 1999, public universities have lost an estimated 5 years to academic strikes. The underlying issues remain unresolved: chronic underfunding, deteriorating infrastructure, and poor welfare for academic staff.
