## Summary

The removal of the decades-old fuel subsidy by President Tinubu in his inaugural address triggered immediate economic shockwaves. Petrol prices tripled overnight, sparking protests across major cities and raising concerns about the impact on Nigeria's poorest citizens.

## Key Points

- Fuel subsidy removal announced on May 29, 2023
- Petrol prices jumped from ₦185 to over ₦550 per liter
- Transport costs doubled within 48 hours
- Food prices surged due to increased transportation costs
- Labor unions threatened nationwide strike action

## Impact

The policy change had immediate and widespread effects:

- **Cost of Living**: Minimum wage became insufficient for basic survival
- **Business**: SMEs faced increased operating costs
- **Social Unrest**: Protests erupted in Lagos, Abuja, and other major cities
- **Economy**: Short-term inflation expected to push millions into poverty

## Context

Nigeria's fuel subsidy cost over ₦4 trillion annually, consuming resources needed for infrastructure and social services. However, its removal without adequate social safety nets has placed enormous strain on households already facing high unemployment and inflation.
