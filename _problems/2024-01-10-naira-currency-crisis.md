## Summary

The Nigerian naira plummeted to an all-time low against the US dollar in January 2024, trading at over ₦1,600/$1 in the parallel market. The continued depreciation follows the Central Bank's currency float policy and mounting foreign exchange pressures.

## Key Points

- Naira lost over 70% of its value against the dollar in 2023
- Inflation rate climbed to 28.9%, the highest in over 28 years
- Foreign exchange reserves remain under pressure
- Food and transport costs surged, hitting low-income households hardest
- Multiple exchange rate windows persist despite CBN unification efforts

## Impact

The currency crisis has created widespread economic hardship:

- **Cost of Living**: Food prices doubled in many markets, pushing millions into poverty
- **Business**: Import-dependent businesses face existential threats from forex scarcity
- **Healthcare**: Pharmaceutical companies struggle to import raw materials
- **Education**: Families can no longer afford foreign tuition fees

## Context

Nigeria's currency challenges stem from structural issues including oil production declines, rising debt servicing costs, and limited non-oil export revenue. The CBN's policy shifts have created market uncertainty while the underlying production base remains weak.
