## Summary

Armed bandit attacks have intensified across Northwest Nigeria, with coordinated raids on villages in Zamfara and Katsina states resulting in deaths, mass abductions, and large-scale displacement. Security forces have struggled to contain the expanding crisis.

## Key Points

- Over 50 villages attacked in coordinated raids across Zamfara State
- Estimated 200+ civilians killed in June 2023 alone
- Mass kidnappings for ransom continue, with schools primary targets
- Over 30,000 internally displaced persons in makeshift camps
- Forests in the region serve as bandit hideouts

## Impact

The security crisis has devastated communities:

- **Education**: Over 400 schools closed due to fear of abductions
- **Agriculture**: Farmers unable to access farmlands, threatening food security
- **Displacement**: Communities living in overcrowded camps with poor sanitation
- **Economy**: Local markets and trade routes disrupted by insecurity

## Context

Banditry in Northwest Nigeria has evolved from cattle rustling to organized criminal enterprises involving kidnappings, village raids, and control of forest territories. The crisis is compounded by porous borders, limited security presence, and complex socio-economic drivers including poverty and climate change impacts on pastoral livelihoods.
