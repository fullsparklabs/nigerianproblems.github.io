## Summary

Severe flooding across the Niger Delta region have displaced over 15,000 people as of October 2023. The flooding, compounded by oil spill contamination, has destroyed farmland, homes, and water sources across multiple states.

## Key Points

- Heavy seasonal rains and dam releases caused rivers to overflow
- Bayelsa and Rivers states are the worst affected
- Oil spills have contaminated floodwaters, creating health hazards
- Over 200 communities affected across 4 states
- Emergency shelters are overwhelmed with displaced persons

## Impact

The environmental disaster has multiple dimensions:

- **Displacement**: Thousands homeless, living in temporary camps
- **Agriculture**: Rice paddies and cassava farms destroyed before harvest
- **Health**: Cholera and typhoid cases spike in flood-affected areas
- **Livelihood**: Fishing communities lose boats and equipment

## Context

Annual flooding in the Niger Delta is exacerbated by decades of environmental degradation from oil exploration. Poor drainage infrastructure and unplanned settlements increase vulnerability. Climate change has intensified rainfall patterns, making seasonal flooding more severe.
