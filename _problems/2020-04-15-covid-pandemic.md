## Summary

The COVID-19 pandemic exposed severe weaknesses in Nigeria's healthcare infrastructure. With limited ICU beds, testing capacity, and medical supplies, the country struggled to respond effectively to the crisis while implementing lockdowns that devastated informal sector workers.

## Key Points

- First case confirmed in Lagos on February 27, 2020
- Initial testing capacity limited to a single laboratory in Lagos
- Nationwide lockdowns implemented in Lagos, Abuja, and Ogun State
- Healthcare workers lacked adequate PPE in early months
- Economic stimulus package of ₦50 billion announced

## Impact

The pandemic had multi-dimensional effects:

- **Healthcare**: Isolation centers overwhelmed, routine health services disrupted
- **Economy**: Oil price crash coincided with pandemic, double economic shock
- **Education**: Schools closed for months, e-learning inaccessible for most
- **Social**: Domestic violence increased during lockdowns

## Context

Nigeria's healthcare system, already strained before the pandemic, faced unprecedented challenges. The crisis highlighted the need for investment in public health infrastructure, local manufacturing of medical supplies, and social safety nets for vulnerable populations.
