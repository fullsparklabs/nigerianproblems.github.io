## Summary

Nigeria's national power grid suffered a complete system collapse on March 15, 2024, leaving over 200 million citizens without electricity for several days. This marks the fourth total grid collapse since 2022, highlighting the persistent fragility of the country's power infrastructure.

## Key Points

- The grid failure occurred at 2:14 PM WAT due to a disturbance at the Jebba transmission station
- All 12 distribution companies reported total loss of supply
- The collapse affected all major cities including Lagos, Abuja, Kano, and Port Harcourt
- Restoration efforts took approximately 72 hours for major lines
- Economic losses estimated in the billions of naira due to halted business operations
- Hospitals were forced to rely on expensive diesel generators for emergency services

## Impact

The power collapse had cascading effects across multiple sectors:

- **Healthcare**: Multiple hospitals reported critical care disruptions as backup generators struggled with fuel shortages
- **Commerce**: Banks, businesses, and markets in major commercial centers were paralyzed
- **Education**: Schools and universities with e-learning infrastructure faced complete shutdowns
- **Security**: Extended blackouts in urban areas raised security concerns

## Context

Nigeria's power infrastructure has been plagued by decades of underinvestment, poor maintenance, and systemic management issues. Despite generating over 4,000 MW of capacity on a good day, the country struggles to distribute power reliably to its 200+ million population.
