## Summary

The #EndSARS movement erupted into Nigeria's largest youth-led protest movement, demanding an end to police brutality and systemic reform of the Special Anti-Robbery Squad (SARS). The protests culminated in the tragic Lekki Toll Gate shooting on October 20, 2020.

## Key Points

- Protests began October 1, 2020, following video evidence of SARS brutality
- Millions of young Nigerians participated in peaceful demonstrations nationwide
- Five key demands presented to the federal government
- SARS officially disbanded by government, but protesters demanded more
- Lekki Toll Gate shooting: military allegedly fired on peaceful protesters

## Impact

The movement had profound social and political consequences:

- **Social Media**: Nigeria's largest digital mobilization in history
- **International Attention**: Global solidarity protests in major cities worldwide
- **Policy**: Police reform committees established, but implementation questioned
- **Trust**: Deepened youth distrust in government and institutions

## Context

#EndSARS represented decades of accumulated frustration with police brutality, unemployment, and poor governance. The movement was notable for its decentralized, leaderless structure and effective use of social media for coordination and documentation.
